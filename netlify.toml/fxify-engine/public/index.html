SEO

SEO Guide

Keywords · meta · internal links · readability

Essential

Use your keyword naturally — don't force it

Primary keyword should appear in the title, first paragraph, at least one H2, and naturally throughout the body. The editor tracks this in the right panel. Target 8–12 occurrences for a 900-word article.

Keep sentences under 25 words

Long sentences hurt readability scores and reader attention. The editor flags sentences over the limit with an amber underline. Break them up.

Add 2–4 internal links per article

Link to related FXIFY blog posts and programme pages. The right panel suggests which articles to link based on the topic. Use descriptive anchor text — not "click here".

Target SEO score above 85

The editor scores your article live. Below 80 Liam will likely send it back. Check the left sidebar before submitting.

GEO

GEO Guide

Writing for ChatGPT · Perplexity · Google AI Overviews

Important

Answer the question in the first paragraph

Search and answer engines look for the direct answer first. Don't bury it. If the article is about "how to pass an evaluation", the second or third sentence should give the core answer. Build the detail after.

Define key terms clearly

Search and answer engines surface definitions prominently. When you use a specific term like "trailing drawdown" or "funded account", define it clearly in one sentence. This dramatically improves GEO score.

Write confident, factual claims — no hedging

Avoid "some traders may find..." or "it could be argued...". Favour direct, citable statements. Write like FXIFY knows what it's talking about — because it does.

Target GEO score above 80

GEO score below 75 will be flagged by Liam in his SEO review. The most common failure points are missing definitions and no direct answer in the intro.

AEO

AEO Guide

Featured snippets · People Also Ask · Voice search

Advanced

Write a direct answer in 40–60 words

Featured snippets are usually 40–60 words. Write one clean, self-contained answer to the main question near the top of the article. This is what Google will pull for the snippet and what voice assistants will read aloud.

Cover PAA questions with H3 subheadings

People Also Ask questions are shown in the brief. Each one should become an H3 heading in the article with a 2–3 sentence answer directly below it. The FAQ section at the end of the article is the right place for these.

Target AEO score above 75

Schema markup is added automatically on publish. Your job is to cover the PAA questions and write the direct answer block. These two things alone move the AEO score significantly.

Quick tips

How to Pass Your Fxify Evaluation First Time

Most traders don't fail their Fxify evaluation because of bad strategy. They fail because they ignore the rules. Here's how to make sure that isn't you.

Key takeaways

  • Know your daily drawdown limit before you place a single trade
  • Size positions at 0.5–1% risk per trade during the evaluation
  • Wait for A-grade setups — you don't need to trade every day
  • The evaluation rewards consistency, not big wins

The evaluation isn't hard because the markets are unpredictable. It's hard because most traders ignore the one thing that matters — risk management. You can have a solid strategy and still blow your Fxify evaluation in two bad trades. The traders who pass aren't necessarily the best traders. They're the most disciplined ones.

This guide covers exactly what you need to do — and what you need to stop doing — to pass your Fxify evaluation on the first attempt.

Know Your Numbers Before You Trade

Before you open your trading platform, write down three numbers: your daily drawdown limit, your maximum drawdown, and your profit target. Know them cold. These aren't guidelines — they're the rules of the game, and breaking any one of them ends your evaluation immediately.

For the Fxify standard two-phase programme, you're working with a 5% daily drawdown and 10% maximum drawdown. That means on a $100,000 account, you can lose no more than $5,000 in a single day. Most traders who breach this limit do so because they kept adding to a losing position rather than cutting it when the signals were clear.

Insert chart or image here

Size Your Positions to Survive Bad Days

Position sizing is where most evaluation attempts die. The pressure of the profit target makes traders over-leverage. They take 3–5% risk on a single trade trying to hit the target faster. One bad setup wipes out two days of gains and suddenly you're chasing losses.

During your Fxify evaluation, keep risk per trade between 0.5% and 1% of your account. On a $100K account that's $500–$1,000 per trade. It feels conservative. It's meant to. The prop firm evaluation is not the place to swing for the fences.

"If you're at 60% of your daily drawdown limit, the answer is never to add to the position. Cut your size in half and protect what you have left."

If you hit 50% of your daily drawdown limit before noon, stop trading for the day. This single rule alone will save more evaluations than any strategy improvement.

Trade Your Best Setups — Nothing Else

The evaluation window creates psychological pressure. You feel like you need to be in the market every day to hit the profit target in time. You don't. A two-week Fxify evaluation with ten trading days only needs you to average 1% gain per day on a 10% target. That's achievable with three or four clean trades per week.

Define your A-grade setup before the evaluation starts. Write it down. Only take trades that match that setup exactly. If you're staring at the charts looking for something to trade, that's a signal to close the platform and come back tomorrow.

What counts as an A-grade setup?

  • Clear market structure — higher highs and higher lows for longs
  • Setup aligns with the higher timeframe trend
  • Entry at a key level — support, resistance, or previous high/low
  • Risk-to-reward of at least 1:2 before you enter

Track Your Progress Daily

Successful evaluation traders treat it like a job, not a game. At the end of each session, record your P&L, how many trades you took, whether each trade matched your A-grade setup, and your current drawdown status. This takes five minutes. It keeps you honest and stops emotional drift from creeping in on day three or four.

Many traders who fail their second or third Fxify evaluation do so because they made the same mistake as the first time. A simple trading log makes those patterns visible.

If you want to understand exactly how the drawdown rules work before you start, read our guide on how drawdown works on a funded account. Getting this wrong is the single most common reason evaluations fail.

The Day Before Your Evaluation Starts

Most traders focus entirely on the evaluation itself and ignore the preparation. The 24 hours before matter. Review the Fxify rules one more time. Set your position size calculator. Mark the key levels on your charts for the week ahead. Know what economic events are scheduled — check the forex trading sessions guide for timing across markets.

Go in prepared, not hopeful.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the Fxify evaluation take?

There is no fixed time limit on the Fxify evaluation. You trade until you hit your profit target, provided you stay within the drawdown rules. Most traders complete it within 14–30 trading days.

What happens if I fail the evaluation?

You can purchase a reset or start a new evaluation. Some Fxify programmes offer a free retry if you breach the rules on day one. Check your specific programme terms before you start.

Can I trade news events during the evaluation?

Yes, unless your programme specifically prohibits it. However, high-impact news events significantly increase volatility and drawdown risk. Most experienced prop firm traders avoid the 15 minutes either side of major releases during their evaluation.
